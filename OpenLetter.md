# An Open Letter to the People Who Turned Cicada 3301 Into a Hackathon Flyer

Cicada 3301 was one of the last genuine mysteries the internet ever produced.

For three years, an unknown group posted puzzles so sophisticated they required knowledge of number theory, steganography, medieval literature, Tor networking, and half a dozen dead languages. They placed posters in 14 cities on 5 continents. They never sold anything. They never revealed themselves. They signed everything with a PGP key that remains unattributed to this day. Governments took interest. Researchers published papers. Communities formed around the shared obsession of trying to understand what it was and who was behind it.

It inspired people because it was real in a way the internet rarely is. No brand. No product. No pitch deck. No sponsor logos. No "powered by." Just a puzzle, a cicada, and silence.

And now some guy in San Francisco stapled a printout to a telephone pole outside the Caltrain station to promote his hackathon.

Same cicada drawing. Same white paper. Same QR code. The whole aesthetic, lifted wholesale. Except instead of leading to an unknowable depth, it leads to a Next.js app on Vercel with Google Analytics and a "powered by Firecrawl" footer. Instead of an anonymous group that has evaded identification for 14 years, we found the founder on LinkedIn in 6 minutes. Instead of RSA, prime number theory, and steganography, we got hex encoding and base64 - the cryptographic equivalent of pig latin.

You took something people genuinely believed in - a myth that made the internet feel bigger and stranger and more alive - and turned it into growth hacking.

Cicada 3301 worked because nobody was selling anything. The moment you attach sponsors, a landing page, and a hackathon registration, you kill the thing that made it sacred. You can copy the wings but you can't copy the mystery. The mystery was that nobody knew why it existed. Yours exists to get sign-ups.

The poster looked right. The puzzle was actually decent. The honeypot was clever. But the soul was missing, and it was missing because it was never there to begin with. It was marketing wearing the skin of something that mattered.

---

*Written March 25, 2026, after cracking the entire puzzle in one evening and tracing it back to its source in 6 minutes.*
